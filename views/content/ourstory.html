Our Story

With research pointing to the fact that 80% of the brain development takes place by the age of 5 you cannot start early enough. Our Mommy and Me programme is designed keeping in mind the kind of exposure children require to develop their Motor Skills and their creativity, giving them an exposure with loads of activities along with their mommies to instil in them that learning is fun and learning happens through all their senses.

Our Vision

Pre Nursery is the first exposure in the long educational journey of every child. It is here that the seeds of a happy schooling are sowed. Thus Pre Nursery is an all important year. At Weavers we will ensure this journey has the right beginning and is steeped in engaging activities to develop a positive attitude towards learning. Children will learn about the world around them and the basic numbers and letters of the alphabet. Music and movement forms a large part of the pre nursery day Large fine and motor development also happens with the help of our large number of outdoor and indoor activities.

Our Mission

  • To frame a developmentally appropriate curriculum that respects and nurtures every child
  • To adopt a dynamic approach that expose children to learning experiences in academics, art, music, games and sports and to a variety of cultures.
  • To ignite children’s intellectual capacities in all areas to make most of the preschool years that will support them on their journey of learning.
  • To help them build caring and trusting relationships thus creating a strong emotional and social base.

We Believe

At this age children start becoming social and enjoy the company of their friends and interact more with each other. Social development is stepped up and children are engaged in small and large group activities. This helps create an environment for learning the nuances of social behaviour – manners, etiquettes and acceptable and unacceptable behaviour. They start exploring various mediums to express their creativity. Information is provided for a deeper understanding of things around them. As they learn and sing new songs and rhymes during music and movement they also explore musical instruments that create different sounds. Pre-reading and pre-writing activities become complex and pre-mathematics is added.
